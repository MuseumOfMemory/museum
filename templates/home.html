01 — Why this archive exists

A record kept by hand,
one person at a time.

For more than a decade, families across Venezuela have searched for the people they love — through prison gates, across borders, into silence. Many have been told to forget. Many have refused.

Memory of Venezuela exists to keep their record. Each profile is built from court documents, family testimony and the words of those who have lived it. Nothing is dramatized. Nothing is invented. Names are added only with the consent of the person or their family.

We are the only organization documenting all three at once — the people still imprisoned, the families and exiles who carry the weight outside, and those who have come home.

06 — About this work

We are the only organization in the world keeping a full record of Venezuela's political prisoners, the families who wait for them, and those who have come home.

Memory of Venezuela is an independent, non-partisan archive. We do not endorse parties or candidates in any country. We document state repression as a matter of public record. Our methodology, sources, and ethics policy are published openly.
