01 — Why this archive exists

A record kept by hand,
one person at a time.

For more than a decade, families across Venezuela have searched for the people they love — through prison gates, across borders, into silence. Many have been told to forget. Many have refused.

The Museum of Memory exists to keep their record. Each profile is built from court documents, family testimony and the words of those who have lived it. Nothing is dramatized. Nothing is invented. Names are added only with the consent of the person or their family.

We are the only organization documenting all three at once — the people still imprisoned, the families and exiles who carry the weight outside, and those who have come home.

05 — How to help

Three things you can do today

01 — Write

Write a letter to a prisoner

Letters reach the families, who carry them in. Even when censors hold them, the families know that someone, somewhere, has written their name. We provide the addresses, the templates, and the translations.

Start a letter →
02 — Share

Share a person's story

The archive lives on attention. Share a profile to your audience, your church, your union, your group chat. Each profile has a clean shareable card and a short version in both languages.

Get a story to share →
03 — Sustain

Sustain the archive

This archive is built by hand by a small staff in Caracas and Miami. A monthly gift of any size keeps the work going — verification, family interviews, legal review, translation.

06 — About this work

We are the only organization in the world keeping a full record of Venezuela's political prisoners, the families who wait for them, and those who have come home.

The Museum of Memory is an independent, non-partisan archive. We do not endorse parties or candidates in any country. We document state repression as a matter of public record. Our methodology, sources, and ethics policy are published openly.
